# Wolf Grammar Quest - Game Overview

A wolf-themed educational game for learning Year 5 UK English grammar and punctuation.

---

## What Is Wolf Grammar Quest?

Wolf Grammar Quest is a calm, focused learning game where you complete grammar challenges to build your wolf pack. Each grammar territory you master rewards you with a unique wolf to add to your collection.

**Designed for:** 10-year-olds learning Year 5 UK English grammar
**Theme:** Wolves and wolf pack building
**Style:** Relaxed learning with no frantic timers

---

## How to Play

### Starting the Game

You begin with **Luna**, your Alpha wolf who leads your pack. From the home screen (Territory Map), you can:

- See all 8 grammar territories
- Track which territories you have completed
- View your best scores
- Access your wolf pack and treat inventory

### Completing a Territory

1. **Choose a territory** from the map
2. **Answer 10+ grammar questions** - all sentences are wolf-themed
3. **Get immediate feedback** after each answer
4. **Learn from mistakes** - wrong answers include detailed explanations
5. **See your final score** and earn rewards

### Earning Wolves

Score **70% or higher** on a territory to earn that territory's wolf. Each wolf has:

- A unique **role** (Scout, Tracker, Hunter, etc.)
- **Five stats** that reflect their role
- A real **wolf fact** to learn

- Uncompleted territories are available to attempt

### Wolf Detail Screen

### Inventory Screen

View your treat collection:

## Scoring Rules

### Question Scoring

- Each correct answer: +1 point
- Each wrong answer: 0 points
- No penalties for wrong answers (you still learn!)

### Territory Completion

- **Below 70%**: No wolf earned, can try again
- **70-89%**: Earn the territory wolf + small treat bonus
- **90%+**: Earn the territory wolf + large treat bonus + special treat

### Replaying Territories

You can replay any territory to:

- Improve your best score
- Earn more treats
- Practice grammar concepts

---

## Question Types

### Multiple Choice

Select the correct answer from 4 options.

_Example:_ "Which word shows possession in: 'The wolf's howl echoed through the mountains'?"

- wolf's (correct)
- howl
- echoed
- mountains

### Additional Types (Future)

- **Tap**: Tap a word in a sentence
- **Tap Clause**: Tap multiple words forming a clause
- **Fill**: Fill in the blank with the correct word

## Learning Features

### Immediate Feedback

After every answer, you see whether you were right or wrong immediately. No waiting until the end.

### Rich Wrong-Answer Explanations

When you get an answer wrong, you receive:

- A brief note about the correct answer
- A **detailed explanation** teaching the grammar concept
- Examples to help you understand

This "learning at the moment of need" approach helps the concept stick.

### Wolf-Themed Content

All sentences use wolf-themed content with natural wolf behaviours:

- "The pack's territory stretched across the valley"
- "The alpha wolf couldn't find the lost cub"
- "Wolves' howls echoed through the night"

## Tips for Success

1. **Read carefully** - Many grammar questions test small details like apostrophe placement
2. **Learn from mistakes** - The wrong-answer explanations are designed to teach you
3. **Try territories more than once** - You can improve your score and earn more treats
4. **Build your pack** - Each wolf has unique stats that may be useful later
5. **Collect all 8 wolves** - Complete every territory at 70%+ to earn the full pack

## Wolf Facts

Each wolf comes with a real fact about wolves:

- **Luna (Alpha)**: The alpha wolf leads and protects the pack.
- **Scout**: Wolves can run up to 40 mph in short bursts!
- **Tracker**: Wolves have about 200 million scent cells - humans have only 5 million!
- **Hunter**: A wolf pack can travel 30 miles in a single day.
- **Guardian**: Wolf pups are born deaf and blind but grow quickly.
- **Howler**: A wolf's howl can be heard up to 10 miles away!
- **Shadow**: Wolves can swim distances of up to 8 miles.
- **Elder**: Wolves can live up to 13 years in the wild.
- **Storyteller**: Wolves use body language, scent, and sounds to communicate with their pack.
